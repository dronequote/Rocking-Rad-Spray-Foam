Spray foam insulation in Oklahoma costs between **$0.44 and $1.60 per board foot** installed, depending on the foam type, thickness, and project scope. For a typical Oklahoma home attic, that translates to **$3,600–$8,000**. For a 30x50 metal building, expect **$6,000–$9,600**.

## What Determines Your Spray Foam Cost

Three factors drive the price of any spray foam project:

### 1. Foam Type

**Open cell spray foam** runs $0.44–$0.65 per board foot. It's the budget-friendly option with solid R-3.7 per inch performance. Best for attics and interior walls.

**Closed cell spray foam** runs $1.00–$1.60 per board foot. Higher R-value (R-6.5+ per inch), built-in vapor barrier, and structural reinforcement. The right choice for metal buildings, crawl spaces, and moisture-prone areas.

### 2. Thickness

Different applications require different thicknesses:
- **Attics:** 5.5 inches (open cell) or 3 inches (closed cell)
- **Walls:** 3.5 inches fills a standard 2x4 cavity
- **Metal buildings:** 2 inches of closed cell is standard
- **Crawl spaces:** 2 inches of closed cell minimum

### 3. Square Footage and Access

Larger projects cost less per board foot because setup and travel are fixed costs. Difficult access (tight crawl spaces, steep roofs) can add 10–20% to the project cost.

## The 30% Federal Tax Credit

The Inflation Reduction Act provides a **30% federal tax credit up to $1,200 per year** for qualifying insulation installed in your primary residence. Spray foam qualifies. On a $5,000 attic job, that's $1,200 back at tax time.

This credit applies to both materials and labor. It's not a deduction — it's a dollar-for-dollar credit that reduces your tax bill directly.

## Spray Foam vs. the Alternatives

| | Spray Foam | Fiberglass Batts | Blown-In Cellulose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upfront cost** | Higher | Lower | Moderate |
| **R-value per inch** | 3.7–7.0 | 3.1–3.8 | 3.2–3.8 |
| **Air sealing** | Yes | No | Partial |
| **Lifespan** | Permanent | 15–20 years | 20–30 years |
| **Annual energy savings** | 30–50% | 10–20% | 15–25% |

The upfront cost of spray foam is higher, but the energy savings are dramatically larger because spray foam actually air-seals the building envelope. Fiberglass and cellulose insulate but don't seal — wind and air infiltration bypass them constantly.

Over 10 years, spray foam typically costs less than fiberglass when you factor in energy savings and the fact that it never needs replacing.
